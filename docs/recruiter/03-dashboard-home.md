# 3. Your dashboard home

Home answers one question: **what needs me today?** It's built to be read in thirty seconds each morning — numbers, then the attention block, then a glance at the live feed.

## 3.1 The KPI tiles

The tiles across the top are live counts — total workers, pending verifications, compliance issues, active projects and the rest. Two things make them more than decoration:

- **Every tile is a door.** Clicking a tile opens the Workers table (or the relevant page) **pre-filtered to exactly that number** — click "7 compliance issues" and you're looking at those seven workers, ready to act. If a number surprises you, click it; never go hunting manually for what a tile already counted.
- **They're current, not yesterday's report.** A verification you complete now moves the numbers now.

## 3.2 The attention block — your triage list

Below the tiles, the attention block surfaces the specific items that most need a human: cards expiring imminently, workers stuck without verification, threads gone stale. Each item carries its one-click action.

How it behaves — worth understanding so it doesn't surprise you:

- **Acting on an item clears it.** Click through, do the thing, it's gone.
- **Dismissing without acting only snoozes it.** Items you wave away **come back** after a cooldown appropriate to their urgency — an expiring safety-critical card returns sooner than a mild profile nudge. This is deliberate: the block's job is to be un-ignorable about things that genuinely matter, while not nagging you twice an hour.
- **An empty attention block is a real signal.** It means nothing urgent is known — enjoy it.

## 3.3 The live activity feed

A rolling stream of what's happening across the platform right now: uploads landing, verifications completing, workers joining projects. It's ambient awareness, not a to-do list — nothing in the feed requires action (anything that does will be in the attention block or your notifications). Its best use: noticing momentum ("uploads are pouring in after that site induction — the queue will grow this afternoon") and spotting your colleagues' work in real time so you don't double-handle.

## 3.4 A morning routine that works

1. Tiles — anything unexpected? Click the surprise.
2. Attention block — clear it, top to bottom. It's ordered by urgency.
3. Verify badge — if the queue is fat, book yourself a queue session; workers are waiting on it (a new worker literally can't progress past their first tier until someone verifies their first document).
4. Messages — clear **Needs you**.

Do that daily and nothing on this platform ever gets old enough to become a problem.
