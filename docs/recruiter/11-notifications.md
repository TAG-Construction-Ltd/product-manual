# 11. Notifications

TAG's notifications are an **action queue, not a news feed** — the design rule is that a notification exists only if there's something for *you* to do about it. Anything merely informational lives in the dashboard's activity feed instead. Internalise that split and the bell stays useful forever.

## 11.1 The bell

Top right, three states: quiet (nothing for you), a count (items waiting), and an urgent treatment when something can't wait. Click for the **quick peek** — the top items, each with its one-tap action inline. Most days, the peek is all you need: open, act, act, done.

## 11.2 The full page

**See all** opens the notifications page: every open item, grouped by category, oldest debts surfaced. Work it like the queue it is — every row is a verb.

## 11.3 Snoozing

Not now ≠ never. **Snooze** an item and pick when it returns — later today, tomorrow, next week. Snoozing is honest deferral and the platform respects it; simply ignoring an item is not, and genuinely urgent classes of item will re-surface through the attention block regardless. Snooze deliberately, don't ignore accidentally.

## 11.4 Notifications vs the other surfaces

| Surface | Job |
|---|---|
| **Notifications** | Things *you* must act on |
| **Attention block** (Dashboard) | The platform's own triage of what most needs a human today |
| **Activity feed** | Ambient awareness — no action implied |
| **Messages: Needs you** | Conversations awaiting your reply |

Overlap is deliberate for the truly urgent (an expiring safety-critical card may appear in more than one) — acting once clears it everywhere.

## 11.5 Tuning

Your account settings ([Chapter 12](12-account-admin.md)) control delivery preferences — what reaches you by email versus in-app only. Tune it so that email = "would interrupt lunch for this"; everything else belongs in the bell.
