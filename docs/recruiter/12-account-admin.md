# 12. Your account & administration

## 12.1 My Account

The account menu (top right) opens your settings, organised into sections:

| Section | What you manage |
|---|---|
| **Profile** | Your name, photo, contact details — what colleagues and audit entries show |
| **Account & security** | Password change; sign-in related settings |
| **Notifications** | Per-category delivery preferences — in-app vs email ([Chapter 11](11-notifications.md)) |
| **Preferences** | Personal defaults for how the dashboard behaves for you |
| **Team** | *Admins:* the team roster and invitations (12.2) |
| **Support** | Help routes and this manual |

Change your password immediately if you ever suspect it's known — remember every action in the platform carries your name into the permanent record.

## 12.2 Team management — admins

Admins manage who has access:

- **Invite** a new team member by email; they receive the sign-up link ([Chapter 2](02-getting-started.md)). Invite with the correct role from the start.
- **Roles:** the working split is **recruiter** (the full daily toolset) vs **admin** (everything, plus team management and the audit trail's Recruiter investigate scope). The complete matrix is in the [Appendices](15-appendices.md).
- **Deactivate** promptly when someone leaves — access removal is part of offboarding, same day, no exceptions. Their history remains in the audit trail (immutably, as ever); deactivation removes future access, never past accountability.

## 12.3 Admin habits that keep the platform healthy

- **Quarterly access review** — does everyone with access still need it, at the role they hold?
- **Watch the Recruiter audit scope occasionally** — not surveillance, stewardship: it's how you spot a teammate drowning in queue volume, or training gaps showing up as repeated corrections.
- **Own the demo/test data** — if test records exist in the environment, keep them obviously named and periodically cleaned.
