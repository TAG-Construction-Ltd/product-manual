# Messages

Messages is where your recruiter reaches you — and where the app makes fixing things fast.

## Two kinds of message

**Normal messages** — plain chat. Read, reply, like any messaging app. You can type or use the **mic** to speak your reply.

**Yellow action cards** — the important ones. When your recruiter needs something specific — a better photo of a card, your date of birth confirmed, the back of a document — it arrives as a **yellow card with one big button**. The button takes you *exactly* where the job is: tap it, do the thing, done. No hunting through menus.

The golden rule: **when a yellow card arrives, tap the button rather than typing a reply.** Doing the action is the answer — the recruiter sees it's done the moment you finish, without you writing a word.

## What messages are about

Most messages connect to something real on your profile — a specific card, a document, your details. The message says which one, so you're never guessing. If a recruiter asks about "your CPCS card," the yellow card's button opens *that card's* screen.

## Response habits that keep you working

- **Same-day beats perfect.** A quick fix today keeps you deployable; a perfect fix on Friday might not.
- **If you can't do it right now**, a one-line reply — "on nights, will sort tomorrow" — keeps everyone happy.
- **If you don't understand what's being asked**, say so in the thread. Plain question, plain answer.

## Notifications

The app pings you when a message or action arrives. Leave notifications on for messages at least — a re-upload request you don't see for a week is a week your profile sat weaker than it needed to be. You can tune what pings you in [Settings](help-account.md).
