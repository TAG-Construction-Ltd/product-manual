# Your cards

Your cards are your proof — CSCS, CPCS, NPORS, IPAF, PASMA, safety certificates, the lot. This page covers their whole life in the app: uploading, getting checked, fixing rejections, and keeping them in date.

## Uploading a card

**Profile → Cards → Add card.** Photograph the front; the app asks for the back when it needs it.

The photo rules, worth repeating because they prevent almost every rejection:

- **Good light** — daylight or a bright room, not the van at dusk
- **Flat on a table** — not held in your hand
- **Whole card in frame** — all four corners
- **No glare** — tilt slightly if light bounces off the plastic
- **In focus** — tap the card on screen to focus before shooting

The app reads the card automatically — the type, your name, the numbers, the dates. You don't type any of that.

## "Being checked" { #being-checked }

After upload, the card shows **"being checked."** A real recruiter is confirming the card is genuine, readable, and yours. **There is nothing more for you to do** — no need to message anyone or upload it again. It usually clears within a day.

While a card is being checked it doesn't count yet — your tier moves when the check completes, not when you upload. That's the whole point: everything on your profile is *confirmed*, which is why recruiters trust it.

## Rejected? Fix it fast { #rejected-fix-it-fast }

If a card comes back rejected, the app tells you **exactly why**, in plain words, with what to do about it:

| The reason says | Do this |
|---|---|
| Photo wasn't clear enough | Retake: better light, flat, in focus. Re-upload. |
| We need the back too | Photograph the back, upload both sides. |
| This card has run out | Renew the card in real life, then upload the new one. |
| Details don't match | Check the card is *yours* and your profile details are right — fix whichever is wrong. |
| Wrong document | Check what was asked for and upload that card. |

Tap **Re-upload**, send the better photo, and it goes straight back to be checked. A rejection costs you nothing except the retake — it doesn't mark your profile.

## Renewals and expiry

Cards run out — the app watches the dates so you don't have to:

- **Before a card expires**, the app warns you. That's your cue to book the renewal.
- **When you get the new card**, photograph and upload it straight away — you don't need to delete the old one, the app knows the new one takes over.
- **If a card expires** with no renewal uploaded, it stops counting — and if it's your CSCS, your tier drops until the new one is confirmed. Upload the renewal and you climb straight back.

The habit that keeps your profile strong: **the day a new card arrives in the post, it goes in the app.** Thirty seconds, and your profile never has a gap.

## Uploading through your recruiter

Handed your card to a recruiter in person, or emailed a photo? They can put it on your profile for you. It gets checked the same way as anything you upload yourself — you'll see it appear in your cards, "being checked" as usual.
